Comprehension Connections Identity
Identity Who you are — shaped by your experiences, relationships, culture, and beliefs. Text-to-Self When something in a text reminds you of your own life or feelings. Inference A conclusion based on evidence and reasoning — reading "between the lines." Explicit Information stated directly and clearly in the text. Implicit Information suggested or hinted at, not stated directly.

Words carry more than definitions. They carry culture, emotion, history, and identity. Understanding the deeper meaning of words helps us connect with texts — and with each other.

Key Vocabulary Strategies

🔎

Context Clues

Use surrounding words and sentences to figure out meaning.

🌳

Word Roots

Break words into parts (prefixes, roots, suffixes) to unlock meaning.

💭

Connotation

The feeling or association a word carries beyond its dictionary definition.

Identity Words in Context

Read these sentences from identity texts. Notice how each bolded word carries deep meaning:

  • "Her heritage was woven into every dish her grandmother prepared." — Heritage means the traditions, values, and culture passed down through generations.
  • "He felt a deep sense of belonging when he heard his family's language spoken." — Belonging means feeling accepted, connected, and at home.
  • "She wanted to reclaim her name after years of hearing it mispronounced." — Reclaim means to take back something that was lost or taken away.
  • "His resilience showed in the way he kept going despite the challenges." — Resilience means the ability to recover and stay strong through difficulty.
  • "The painting was a mosaic of her many identities — daughter, student, artist, friend." — Mosaic (figurative) means a combination of many different pieces forming one beautiful whole.
Heritage Traditions, values, and culture passed down through generations. Belonging Feeling accepted, connected, and at home in a group or place. Reclaim To take back something that was lost, dismissed, or taken away. Resilience The ability to recover and stay strong through difficulty. Mosaic A combination of many different pieces forming one beautiful whole (figurative). Connotation The emotional feeling or association a word carries beyond its definition.

Theme & Message

Learn the difference between theme and topic. Identify the deeper messages authors communicate about identity.

🎯 Learning Targets

  • I can explain the difference between theme and topic.
  • I can identify the theme of a text about identity.
  • I can support my theme statement with evidence from the text.
ThemeText EvidenceAnalysis
Topic The subject a text is about — can be stated in 1-2 words (e.g., "identity," "friendship"). Theme The deeper message or lesson the author communicates about the topic — stated as a full sentence. Universal Theme A theme that applies across cultures and time periods, like "true strength comes from being yourself."
🚀

≈ 3 min
☐▼

📺 Video — Theme vs. Topic (4 min)

Topic vs. Theme — What's the Difference?

  • Topic: What the text is about, in 1-2 words. Example: identity
  • Theme: The deeper message the author is teaching. Example: "Embracing your true identity takes courage but leads to belonging."

Narrative Structure

Learn how stories are built using the plot diagram. Map the structure of identity narratives.

🎯 Learning Targets

  • I can identify the 5 parts of a plot diagram.
  • I can map a narrative onto a plot structure.
  • I can explain how structure creates tension and meaning.
Plot StructureNarrativeAnalysis
Exposition The beginning — introduces characters, setting, and the situation before the conflict. Rising Action Events that build tension and develop the conflict. Climax The turning point — the moment of greatest tension or the key decision. Falling Action Events after the climax as the conflict starts to resolve. Resolution The ending — how the story wraps up and what has changed.
🚀

📺 Video — Plot Diagram (5 min)

The 5 Parts of a Plot Diagram

  1. Exposition: Meet the characters and setting. Everything is "normal."
  2. Rising Action: Problems build. Tension increases.
  3. Climax: The turning point — the biggest moment of tension.
  4. Falling Action: The conflict begins to settle.
  5. Resolution: The story ends. What has changed?

Anchor Text: "The Name Jar" — Plot Map

1️⃣

Exposition

Unhei arrives at a new school. Kids struggle with her name.

2️⃣

Rising Action

She considers choosing an American name. Classmates put suggestions in a jar.

3️⃣

Climax

Unhei decides to keep her real name and tells the class what it means.

4️⃣

Falling Action

Classmates practice saying her name correctly.

5️⃣

Resolution

Unhei feels proud and accepted. She belongs as herself.

Revision & Voice

Strengthen your narrative draft by revising for word choice, sentence variety, and voice. Learn to edit like a real author.

🎯 Learning Targets

  • I can revise my writing to improve word choice and detail.
  • I can vary my sentence structure for rhythm and effect.
  • I can strengthen my writer's voice to make my narrative unique.
RevisionVoiceWord Choice
Voice The unique personality and style that comes through in a writer's words — what makes YOUR writing sound like YOU. Word Choice Selecting specific, vivid words instead of vague ones (e.g., "stomped" instead of "walked"). Sentence Variety Mixing short and long sentences to create rhythm and keep the reader engaged.
🚀

Launch Pad

≈ 3 min
☐▼

Compare these two versions:

Version A: "I was nervous. I went to the front. I talked about my family."

Version B: "My hands trembled as I walked to the front of the room. Twenty pairs of eyes locked on me. I took a breath, and began to tell the story my grandmother had passed down — the one about the river where she grew up."

📚

Learn

≈ 10 min
☐▼
📺 Video — Revising Your Writing (5 min)

3 Revision Power Moves:

  1. Show, Don't Tell: Replace "I was scared" with "My knees locked. My mouth went dry."
  2. Power Words: Swap vague words for precise ones — "walked" → "shuffled," "nice" → "welcoming"
  3. Sentence Variety: Mix short punchy sentences with longer, flowing ones. Short = impact. Long = detail and reflection.

Discussion Circle Guidelines:

  1. Share: Read your narrative (or a favourite excerpt) aloud with expression.
  2. Listen: Give the speaker your full attention — no interrupting.
  3. Respond: After each share, offer one genuine compliment and one thoughtful question.
  4. Respect: Every story matters. Treat each narrative with care.
👍
